A little about myself...

I am a 3rd year college student attending UCR. I am majoring in pre-medical biological sciences and minoring in general business. To be fair, I plan on studying medicine in the future. This particular field is known for being very expensive, but I strongly feel that it is my passion. My fascination with the study of medicine is definitely not random, as I have always wanted to help people. I believe that there is a need for more female OB/GYNs in the world. More specifically, there is a need for more female African Americans in the medical field, as we make up approximately less than 3% of the active physicians in the world today. My plan is to open my own Labor & Delivery practice while also receiving my MD. I want to create a safe space for women to go and receive personal and professional care.

From a young age, I was taught to know and value my worth. To appreciate my dark skin, unruly hair, and naturally curved body. As an afro-Caribbean woman growing up in the United States, it was often hard for me to feel represented. My fascination with the study of medicine is definitely not random, as I have always wanted to help people. I think the female reproductive system is fascinating, as well as the ever-evolving process of childbirth. However, my interest in this particular aspect of medicine is rooted in something deeper: the need for more female OB/GYNs. More specifically, the need for more women of color in the medical field, making substantial differences in the world.
